# 🔥 Interview Hard Questions — Prime, Sieve, Factorisation

## Question 1: Largest Component Size by Common Factor
- **Platform:** LeetCode
- **Link:** https://leetcode.com/problems/largest-component-size-by-common-factor/
- **Difficulty:** Hard
- **Description:** Given an array of unique positive integers, connect nums[i] and nums[j] if they share a common factor > 1. Return the size of the largest connected component.
- **Key Concepts:** Prime Factorisation, Union-Find (DSU), Sieve-like decomposition
- **Why Interview-Worthy:** Combines prime factorisation with DSU — a perfect cross-topic hard problem.

## Question 2: Count Primes in Ranges (Segmented Sieve)
- **Platform:** Codeforces
- **Link:** https://codeforces.com/problemset/problem/776/B (Sherlock and his girlfriend)
- **Difficulty:** Hard (conceptual)
- **Alternative:** SPOJ PRIME1 — https://www.spoj.com/problems/PRIME1/
- **Description:** Given range [L, R] where L, R can be up to 10^12, find all primes in that range using Segmented Sieve.
- **Key Concepts:** Sieve of Eratosthenes, Segmented Sieve, Range Queries
- **Why Interview-Worthy:** Tests deep understanding of sieve optimization for large ranges.
